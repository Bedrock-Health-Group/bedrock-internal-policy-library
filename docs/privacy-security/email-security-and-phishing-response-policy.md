## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish Bedrock Health Group's standards for recognizing, reporting, and responding to phishing, suspicious emails, malicious links, unsafe attachments, fraudulent messages, impersonation attempts, and related social engineering activity.

This policy is intended to reduce the risk of unauthorized access, credential theft, financial fraud, malware infection, privacy incidents, protected information exposure, reputational harm, and disruption to Bedrock Health Group systems.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals who access or use Bedrock Health Group email, Microsoft 365, Outlook, Microsoft Teams, websites, portals, devices, shared files, collaboration tools, or related communication systems.

This includes employees, officers, trustees, contractors, volunteers, advisors, committee members, vendors, and other authorized users.

## 3. Policy Statement

Bedrock Health Group requires all covered users to exercise caution when receiving, opening, replying to, forwarding, or acting on emails, Teams messages, attachments, links, login prompts, payment requests, document-sharing notices, account alerts, or other electronic communications.

Covered users must not click suspicious links, open unexpected attachments, provide passwords, provide multifactor authentication codes, approve unexpected multifactor authentication prompts, send sensitive information, or complete financial or account-change requests without appropriate verification.

Suspected phishing, impersonation, credential theft, malware, payment fraud, or suspicious communication must be reported promptly through the approved reporting process.

## 4. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Phishing** | A deceptive attempt to obtain sensitive information, credentials, money, or access by pretending to be a legitimate person, organization, system, or service. |
| **Social Engineering** | Manipulation intended to cause a person to disclose information, click a link, approve access, send money, change payment instructions, or take another unsafe action. |
| **Suspicious Message** | An email, Teams message, text message, website prompt, shared-file notice, or other communication that appears unusual, deceptive, unexpected, unsafe, or inconsistent with normal business activity. |
| **Credential Theft** | The unauthorized collection or use of usernames, passwords, multifactor authentication codes, tokens, or other access credentials. |
| **Covered User** | Any person subject to this policy. |

## 5. Phishing Warning Signs

Covered users must treat a message as suspicious when it includes one or more of the following indicators:

- Urgent demands, threats, penalties, rewards, or pressure to act immediately.
- First-time, unfamiliar, infrequent, or external senders.
- Messages from known senders that seem unusual, pressured, poorly timed, or out of character.
- Poor spelling, unusual grammar, awkward wording, unexpected formatting, or inconsistent branding.
- Generic greetings instead of the recipient's name.
- Sender domains that do not match the claimed organization.
- Lookalike domains, misspellings, replaced characters, or suspicious sender addresses.
- Outlook, Microsoft 365, or Teams warnings that the sender cannot be verified.
- Unexpected links, attachments, shared files, invoices, DocuSign-style requests, payment instructions, password reset notices, or account alerts.
- Requests for passwords, MFA codes, bank information, gift cards, wire transfers, protected information, confidential documents, or changes to payment details.
- Links that do not match the visible text or expected destination when inspected.
- Attachments that were not expected or do not match the sender's normal communication pattern.

## 6. Required User Response

If a covered user receives a suspicious email, Teams message, link, attachment, file-sharing notice, login prompt, or request, the user must:

1. Stop and avoid clicking links or opening attachments.
2. Do not reply to the suspicious message.
3. Do not provide passwords, MFA codes, bank details, protected information, confidential information, or payment information.
4. Verify the request using a separate trusted channel, such as a known phone number, official website, or previously established contact method.
5. Report the message using the approved reporting process.
6. Delete the message after reporting, unless instructed to preserve it by Privacy & Security, Governance & Compliance, or an authorized administrator.

## 7. Reporting Procedure

Covered users should report suspected phishing through the appropriate Microsoft reporting tool when available.

For Outlook, users should select the suspicious message and use:

**Report > Report phishing**

For Microsoft Teams, users should use the available message reporting function and select the security risk, spam, phishing, or malicious content option when available.

If Microsoft reporting tools are unavailable, the user must notify Bedrock Health Group's designated security or compliance contact.

Security and compliance concerns may be reported to:

**Bedrock Health Group - Governance & Compliance**

Email: compliance@bedrockhealthgroup.org

## 8. If a User Clicks a Suspicious Link or Provides Information

If a covered user believes they may have clicked a malicious link, opened a malicious attachment, entered credentials, approved an unexpected MFA prompt, shared sensitive information, or interacted with a suspicious website, the user must immediately:

1. Stop using the suspicious site, message, or interaction.
2. Write down key details while fresh, including time, sender, link, attachment name, website, system involved, and information shared.
3. Change affected passwords immediately.
4. Use unique passwords for each affected account.
5. Confirm multifactor authentication is enabled where available.
6. Notify Bedrock Health Group's designated security or compliance contact.
7. Notify any affected financial institution, vendor, partner, or account provider when appropriate.
8. Report financial fraud, identity theft, or criminal activity to the appropriate authority when warranted.
9. Preserve relevant evidence if instructed by Privacy & Security, Governance & Compliance, legal counsel, or an authorized administrator.

## 9. Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Covered Users** | Identify, avoid, verify, and report suspicious messages promptly. |
| **Privacy & Security** | Maintain this policy, support security awareness, review reported threats, and coordinate response. |
| **Governance & Compliance** | Oversee policy publication, compliance alignment, review workflow, and documentation practices. |
| **Managers / Supervisors** | Encourage prompt reporting and prohibit retaliation for good-faith reporting. |
| **IT / Microsoft 365 Administrator** | Support message review, message tracing, account protection, security configuration, and remediation where applicable. |
| **Authorized Reviewers** | Review suspected phishing or compromise reports and recommend corrective action when needed. |

## 10. Technical Safeguards

Bedrock Health Group may use technical safeguards to reduce phishing and email security risk, including:

- Multifactor authentication.
- Strong password requirements.
- Microsoft 365 security controls.
- External sender warnings.
- Anti-phishing and anti-malware protections.
- Restricted administrative access.
- Role-based access controls.
- Audit logs and message tracing.
- User training and awareness activities.
- Incident response and escalation procedures.

## 11. Training and Awareness

Covered users may be required to complete email security, phishing awareness, cybersecurity, privacy, or incident response training.

Training may include examples of phishing indicators, suspicious links, unsafe attachments, impersonation attempts, external sender warnings, business email compromise, credential theft, payment fraud, and proper reporting steps.

## 12. Documentation and Recordkeeping

Bedrock Health Group may maintain records of reported phishing, suspected compromise, remediation steps, user training, access changes, investigations, and related corrective actions.

Records should be maintained in accordance with Bedrock Health Group's applicable retention, compliance, privacy, and security requirements.

## 13. Enforcement

Failure to follow this policy may result in corrective action, additional training, access restrictions, contract remedies, removal of system access, or other action appropriate to the circumstances.

Good-faith reporting of suspected phishing or security concerns is encouraged and must not result in retaliation.

## 14. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy must be approved by Privacy & Security, Governance & Compliance, or another authorized Bedrock Health Group representative.

Exceptions must not create unreasonable risk to Bedrock Health Group systems, data, users, partners, or public trust.
