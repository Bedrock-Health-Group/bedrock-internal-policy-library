## 1. Purpose

Bedrock Health Group (“BHGroup”) is a charitable, tax-exempt organization whose credibility, community trust, and IRS standing depend on strict adherence to ethical standards. Because BHGroup operates within healthcare compliance, public health systems, safety-net ecosystems, and federally regulated environments, the potential for perceived or actual conflicts must be handled with exceptional rigor.

The purpose of this Conflict of Interest Policy is to ensure that all individuals entrusted with governance, review authority, advisory input, decision-making power, or operational influence conduct themselves in a manner that protects BHGroup from private benefit, undue influence, and jeopardy to its exempt status.

This policy establishes the standards, expectations, processes, and safeguards that govern how conflicts are identified, disclosed, evaluated, and managed. It applies to all individuals serving in Bedrock’s governance ecosystem, including the Board of Trustees, Board Officers, Advisory Board, Advisory Review Council (ARC), Community Funding Authorization Committee (CFAC), Governance, Ethics & Compliance Committee (GECC), and executive leadership.

This policy is designed to ensure that decisions are made solely in furtherance of BHGroup’s mission, free from personal bias or competing interests, and consistent with federal tax law, state nonprofit law, and Bedrock’s internal compliance framework.

## 2. Scope and Applicability

This policy applies to every individual who participates in governance or decision-making at Bedrock Health Group. This includes Trustees, officers, advisory members, committee members, key employees, and any contractor or subject-matter expert whose role grants access to nonpublic information, influence over decisions, or involvement in program evaluation, funding recommendations, or policy oversight.

Because Bedrock engages with vulnerable providers, federal systems, and regulated entities, even the appearance of a conflict can undermine trust. Individuals covered by this policy are required to uphold the highest standards of independence and ethical conduct, regardless of whether their role includes voting authority, advisory input, or evaluative responsibility.

## 3. Definitions

A **conflict of interest** exists whenever an individual’s personal, professional, financial, or relational interest could compromise their ability to act solely in the best interest of BHGroup. Conflicts may be actual, potential, or perceived.

An **Interested Person** is any individual subject to this policy who holds a role that influences BHGroup decisions.

An interested person may have a **Financial Interest** if they or a family/household member holds an ownership stake, investment interest, employment relationship, compensation arrangement, or prospective benefit involving an entity that interacts with BHGroup.

Interests may also be **personal or professional**, arising from advisory roles, volunteer positions, partnerships, alliances, or close relationships with applicants or stakeholders.

A **Family Member** includes spouses, domestic partners, parents, siblings, children, and individuals residing in the same household.

A financial or personal interest does not automatically constitute a conflict. The determination of whether a conflict exists is made through the evaluation process defined in this policy.

## 4. Duty to Disclose

All individuals covered by this policy have an affirmative obligation to disclose any relationship, interest, or circumstance that could impair or appear to impair their neutrality. Disclosure is required as soon as the individual becomes aware of the issue, regardless of whether they believe a conflict exists.

Full, timely disclosure is essential. Individuals must provide sufficient detail to enable an accurate evaluation of the situation.

GECC receives and evaluates disclosures for advisory bodies and committees. The Board of Trustees evaluates disclosures involving Trustees, officers, or GECC members.

Failure to disclose is itself considered a breach of fiduciary duty.

## 5. Recusal Requirements

Once a conflict is disclosed, the individual must immediately recuse themselves from all involvement in the matter, including discussions, scoring activities, deliberations, electronic workspaces, and communications.

Recusal applies across every governance body, including:

- **ARC members**, who must not score or review applications involving any conflicting interest.
- **CFAC members**, who must not vote on or influence funding decisions where a conflict exists.
- **Advisory Board members**, who must not provide input on matters affecting conflicted entities or parties.
- **GECC members**, who must not participate in conflict determinations involving themselves.
- **Trustees**, who must not vote on or deliberate in matters involving their own interests or those of close associates.

An individual may not attempt to influence deliberations directly or indirectly once recused.

## 6. Evaluation of Conflicts

GECC evaluates conflicts involving advisory bodies, ARC, CFAC, staff, and subject-matter experts. The Board of Trustees evaluates conflicts involving GECC members or Trustees.

Conflict determinations consider whether the interest:

- Could reasonably affect independent judgment.
- Presents a risk of bias or undue influence.
- Creates the appearance of impropriety.
- Intersects with financial or organizational benefit.
- Could compromise BHGroup’s compliance integrity.
- Could undermine fairness in the Cornerstone Access Fund review process.
- Conflicts with IRS private benefit restrictions.

The absence of financial benefit does not eliminate the possibility of a conflict.

## 7. Management of Conflicts

If GECC or the Board determines that a conflict exists, several measures may be implemented:

1. Full recusal of the individual from all related matters.
2. Assignment of an alternate reviewer, evaluator, or committee member.
3. Restrictions on access to documentation or data related to the matter.
4. Enhanced oversight of independently reviewed materials.
5. Modification of committee structure for that funding cycle if needed to maintain independence.
6. Engagement of external advisors when independence must be fortified.
7. Board review of committee decisions for potential influence.

Where conflicts impact ARC or CFAC processes, GECC may require corrective action, including re-evaluation of scores, re-review of applications, or procedural adjustments.

## 8. Documentation and Recordkeeping

All disclosures, recusals, reviews, determinations, and conflict-related actions must be fully documented.

Meeting minutes must clearly record:

- The nature of the conflict.
- The disclosure made.
- Individuals recused.
- Determinations by GECC or the Board.
- Alternatives considered.
- Final decisions and rationale.
- Votes cast by disinterested members.

GECC maintains a permanent ledger of conflict disclosures, recusal logs, annual certifications, and conflict-related audit trails.

## 9. Compensation and Related-Party Transactions

No individual may participate in or vote on their own compensation or on compensation involving related persons. All compensation decisions must be guided by independent comparability data and approved by disinterested Trustees.

Any transaction involving an entity connected to an interested person is subject to heightened scrutiny, must demonstrably further BHGroup’s charitable purposes, and must not result in private inurement or excessive benefit.

## 10. Annual Certification

All Trustees, officers, advisory members, ARC and CFAC participants, GECC members, and key individuals must complete an annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement affirming that they:

- Have received and reviewed this policy.
- Understand their obligations.
- Will promptly disclose conflicts.
- Will comply with recusal procedures.
- Recognize BHGroup’s 501(c)(3) responsibilities.

Annual certification is a condition of continued service.

## 11. Periodic Reviews

GECC shall conduct periodic evaluations of conflict-of-interest compliance, including:

- Review of ARC and CFAC cycles for conflict integrity.
- Verification that disclosures were properly handled.
- Review of related-party transactions.
- Examination of compensation decisions.
- Assessment of governance structures for ongoing independence.

Findings may be presented to the Board for corrective action or policy refinement.

## 12. Use of Outside Experts

When necessary, BHGroup may engage independent legal counsel, auditors, or compliance professionals to assist in conflict determinations, compensation analysis, policy review, or governance oversight.

Use of experts supports but does not replace the Board’s fiduciary responsibility.
