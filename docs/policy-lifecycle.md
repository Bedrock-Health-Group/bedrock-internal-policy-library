# Policy Lifecycle

Bedrock Health Group policies should move through a controlled lifecycle from request through publication, review, revision, and retirement.

## Lifecycle Stages

| Stage | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Policy content is being prepared and is not approved for operational use. |
| **Under Review** | Policy content is being reviewed by the appropriate owner, reviewer, or approving authority. |
| **Published** | Policy has been approved for publication in the Bedrock Health Group policy library. |
| **Adopted** | Policy has been formally adopted by the appropriate governing authority, when required. |
| **Retired** | Policy is no longer active and should not be used for current operations. |

## Standard Workflow

1. Policy request is submitted.
2. Policy owner is assigned.
3. Public or internal classification is confirmed.
4. Draft is created using the Bedrock policy template.
5. Required reviewers are identified.
6. Policy is reviewed for substance, formatting, sensitivity, and publication readiness.
7. Pull request is opened in GitHub.
8. Required approval is obtained.
9. Policy is merged and published.
10. Policy is reviewed according to its review cycle.

## Review Cycle

Unless another review cycle is specified, Bedrock Health Group policies should be reviewed annually.

Each policy should include:

- Policy ID
- Version
- Status
- Effective date
- Last reviewed date
- Next review due date
- Policy owner
- Approving authority

## Public/Internal Classification

Before publication, every policy must be classified as either public-facing or internal-only.

Public-facing policies may be published in this public library.

Internal-only policies should be maintained in a separate private/internal policy environment.

## Retirement

Retired policies should not be deleted without review. Where appropriate, they should be marked as retired, removed from public navigation, and retained in version history or internal records.
